    • Research Summary

    My research interest is in scientific computing, particularly the solution of partial differential equations. My goal is to develop efficient methods for computational fluid dynamics capable of capturing the vast range of scales characteristic of turbulent flows. Ideally, an efficient method is accurate, parallelizeable, stable, and fast. My most recent work is in the field of high-order continuous finite element methods for unstructured meshes.

    My past work has only piqued my interest in scientific computation and explored a small selection of the applications. I am interested in continuing work in the development and implementation of solvers for multiphase flows, fluid-structure interactions, simulating the behavior of waves at interfaces, the use of stochastic models in the analysis of non- linear dynamical systems, and the parallelization of solvers for use on both CPU and GPU based clusters. I hope to continue expanding my research portfolio, furthering my ability to find solutions to the systems that describe the world around us while creating visualizations to demonstrate them.

    I am currently working toward building a portfolio of examples to demonstrate proficiency in a few other topics that interest me. These topics include high-performace computing for machine learning, data analytics, and parallel programming on GPU and CPU clusters. To assist, I will be taking part in a summer program. It will focus on the effective exploitation of state-of-the-art hybrid High-Performance Computing (HPC) systems with a special focus on Data Analytics. The content of the course is tailored for intermediate graduate students interested in both learning parallel programming models, and having hands-on experience using HPC systems.

    Interests

    Finite Element Methods

    The finite element method is a divide and conquer approach to numerically approximating the solutions of differential and partial differential equations. The divide and conquer is completed in a variational sense in which the error exists orthogonally to the solution space.

    Personal Philosophy

    I have always believed that the reason to live was to learn the reason to live. This concept ties the pursuit of knowledge directly to life, and whether it be for altruistic or selfish means is irrelevant. To learn is not a luxury that should be enjoyed in leisure, but rather a directive and a motivation that should be fought for at all times. There is no time to spare, and even in these disruptive times, we must continue to try to understand the world inside and around us.

    Ambition, curiousity, resilience, and humility are tracers for those seeking to learn. I do not believe that a formal education is necessary - I believe it can be helpful and harmful. I do not believe that a formal education should only teach us those fundamental topics and tasks that are essential to our career paths, but rather it should teach us to be calm when we know that we do not know. Because true learning can only happen when you finally recognize that are lost. The remaining process is the endless refinement of eternally unclear ideas.
